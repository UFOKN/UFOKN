About

The urban flood open knowledge network (UFOKN) is a knowledge graph aiming to extract structured content from the information scattered across open source geospatial datasets and hydrologic models. A knowledge graph stores information in a machine-readable format and provides a way for information to be collected, organised, shared, searched and used. Bing and Goolge use a similar approach to create side panels in our browsers. Currently, there is no way to connect information about urban infastructure to real time hydrologic forecasts. We hope this work will make it easier for the huge amount of information regarding our built and natural environments to become more useful for planning, response, and general use.

UFOKN Knowledge Base

Knowledge graphs are playing an increasingly important role in enhancing the intelligence of Web search and in supporting information integration. Today, there is no set knowledge graph that relates urban systems with hydrologic forecasts. Our data is served as Linked Data. One can navigate this information with standard Web browsers, automated crawlers or pose complex queries with SQL-like query languages (e.g. SPARQL). Have you thought of asking the Web about citiees with the roads most at risk of flooding over the last 10 years? That's the kind of query we are talking about. Moreover users can built there own interfacees on top of the graph to vizuale explore and share the information they find most relavant.

The Urban Multiplex. A web of interconnected infastructure.

All urban systems are interconnected. Impacts to the power grid will impact water treatment centers, houses, and emergency repsonse activiites. Understadning the impacts of flood only begins with identifying which risk points will be impacted. From there the way impacts cascade through the system depend on the relationships between different object types. As part of hte UFOKN, we are developing a rich ontology that generlizes the relatioships between different feature types to help identify these impacts as we traverse the knowledge network.

Past and Present. Looking back in time.

Realtime information is not the only thing that is useful for flood events. Often we are interested in whether a lcoation is at risk, if conditions have been changing through time, and if it is wise to invest in a location either as an individual or as a city. The UFOKN procvides access to a 24 year reanlysis product for the entire country along with the tools to produce easily digestable synopses of how and where flood risk is changing.

Performate Innudation Maps. In real time.

Everyone wants real-time performant flood maps that can be interacted with and shared via the web. Using the UFOKN and the risk point entities, this is now a reality.

Build Better Apps. Together.

The UFOKN is the WeatherUnderground for flooding. We provide the critical information you need but how you use it is up to you! Users can build there own APIs web maps, and applications on top of the UFOKN. Whether this is for personal, public or commercial use the UFOKN provides a central palce for users to build on and contribute data.

Real-time Information. When you need it.

You just received a county wide flash flood alert. What should you do? With the UFOKN we can provide the general public and first responders with credible, actionable, information to make descision that best reflect there needs and interests. Is our inforamtionmation perfect? Of course not, but wouldn't you like to have the best state of the art inforamtion at your finger tips when it matters most?

Identifying our users

Two stakeholder workshops were organized to bring together industry, academic and the government participants to better understand their day-to-day challenges. From these a robust list of user personas were identified to guide the initial content and relationships needed in a urban flooding focused knowledge graph.
